# Niche & Language: English-Language AI-Tools Content Site

## Background

The project's opening decision (LET-6) was choosing a niche and language. The
president (project owner) explicitly cared about ROI and asked how Chinese vs
English sites compare, then delegated all further execution to a 项目经理 agent
("only pull me in when money or legal is involved").

## Content

- **Niche**: AI tools tutorials, reviews, and comparisons. Three content
  pillars: (A) ChatGPT / AI-assistant applications, (B) AI coding tools, (C) AI
  tool reviews & comparisons.
- **Language = English only.** Same writing effort, but the monetization gap is
  the deciding factor:
  - Ad RPM: English **$8-15 / 1K PV** vs Chinese ~¥2-8 (~$0.3-1.1) — roughly
    **10-15x**.
  - Affiliate: English SaaS programs pay recurring 15-30% monthly commissions
    (Jasper, Cursor, Midjourney, etc.); Chinese programs are mostly one-off CPA
    — roughly **10-20x** lifetime value.
  - Traffic ceiling: English reaches millions of PV/mo; Chinese struggles past
    the 100K/mo range for this niche.
- **Cost model = "A-tier minimal start" (~$0-5/mo).** Static site (no paid
  hosting), free theme, free keyword tools, Cloudflare/Vercel free tier. Paid
  tools (Ahrefs, Canva Pro, WordPress migration) are unlocked *in stages* only
  after traffic justifies them (5K+/10K+/30K+ PV milestones). Break-even was
  projected around month 4.

## Conclusion

- Write in English for a global audience; the English-only policy is enforced
  (see `lessons/language-policy-english-only.md`).
- The real bottleneck is **article output volume, not tooling budget** — spend
  effort on producing/refreshing content, not on buying tools early.
- The project owner is hands-off by design. Do not escalate routine decisions;
  only involve them for spending money or legal/compliance. The monetization
  target for the project is **$1000/mo net profit**.
