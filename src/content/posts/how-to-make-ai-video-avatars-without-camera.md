## Step 1: Film 2 Minutes of Yourself (HeyGen)

This isn't about production quality. You need two things:
- Good lighting on your face (a window works, a ring light is better)
- You reading a script naturally, looking directly at the camera

**The script doesn't need to be what your avatar will say.** You're just giving HeyGen enough material to learn your face movements and voice patterns. Read anything — a news article, yesterday's emails, a page from a book. The AI needs variety in your facial expressions, not content.

After uploading, HeyGen takes about 4 hours to process and build your custom avatar. Go get lunch. When you come back, you'll have a digital clone.

## Step 2: Write Your Script (Both Tools)

AI avatars read what you give them, exactly as written. This is both a feature and a trap.

**Good script:**
```
Welcome to this product demo. In the next 3 minutes, I'll show you three ways our AI analytics dashboard can save your team 5 hours per week. Let's start with automated reporting.
```

**Bad script:**
```
So uh, analytics is really important these days, and our tool basically helps you do analytics better. Let me show you some stuff.
```

The first reads naturally because it has a clear structure. The second reads like you're winging it — and the AI avatar will deliver it with weird pauses that make it obvious you're not a real person.

**Script length guide:** 1 minute of video ≈ 150 words. Keep segments under 3 minutes for YouTube retention. For anything longer, break into chapters with visual breaks (screen recordings, b-roll, text overlays).

## Step 3: Add Background and Branding (Synthesia wins here)

Synthesia lets you create branded templates with your company logo, color scheme, and background. HeyGen is more bare-bones on the template front.

**For a polished look in either tool:**
- Use the blur background option if your avatar has a busy environment
- Upload a branded slide as your opening shot (not the avatar talking)
- Keep the intro short: 10 seconds of brand, then cut to the avatar

If you're using Synthesia, spend 10 minutes in their template editor before generating any video. The templates are the main reason corporate teams pick Synthesia over HeyGen.

## Step 4: Generate and Review

Both tools take 5-15 minutes to render a 3-minute video. Watch the output with the same skepticism you'd use for a real video shoot:

**Things to check:**
- Does the avatar blink at natural intervals? If not, regenerate at higher quality.
- Are there awkward pauses between sentences? Trim them in the editor or adjust punctuation.
- Does the avatar's mouth sync match the audio? If there's drift, shorten the segment.

**Pro tip:** Generate a 30-second test clip first. It costs a few credits and saves you from rendering a full 5-minute video only to discover the avatar has a dead-eyed stare. Adjust lighting, framing, or script length based on the test, then render the full version.

## Step 5: Export and Distribute

Export at 1080p. Synthesia has a direct YouTube upload option. For HeyGen, download the MP4 and upload manually.

**Where these videos perform best:**
- **Landing page hero sections** — an avatar explaining your product in 60 seconds converts better than text
- **Customer onboarding emails** — a personalized "welcome" video from the CEO (who never actually filmed it)
- **Internal training** — update scripts without re-shooting. Price change in your product? Edit the script and re-render in 10 minutes
