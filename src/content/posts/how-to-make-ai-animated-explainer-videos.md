A client asked me for a 2-minute product explainer video. Their budget was $200. Their deadline was tomorrow. I made it in 32 minutes for $29.

The formula: Synthesia generates the talking-head presenter. CapCut adds animated text overlays, transitions, and motion graphics. Together, they produce a video that looks like it cost $1,500 and took a week. Here's the exact workflow.

## Why This Combo Works

Synthesia handles the hard part — a realistic human presenter who delivers your script without filming. CapCut handles the polish — animated text, transitions, lower thirds, and motion effects that make a static talking head look like a produced video.

If you tried this with just Synthesia, the output would be a person talking at a camera for two minutes. That's a webcam video, not an explainer. CapCut turns it into something you'd pay an editor for.

## Step 1: Script It Like a Sales Page, Not a Blog Post

Explainer scripts follow a specific structure that converts better than any other format:

**0:00-0:15 — Hook:** State the problem your viewer has. "You've spent three hours formatting that quarterly report. Your boss asked for it twice already. And you still have 12 slides to go."

**0:15-1:00 — Solution intro:** Introduce the product as the answer. Not features. Transformation. "Our dashboard auto-generates quarterly reports in under 2 minutes. Here's how."

**1:00-1:45 — Three key features:** Show, don't tell. Each feature gets a visual demonstration (screen recording in CapCut) while the Synthesia avatar narrates.

**1:45-2:00 — CTA:** One clear action. "Start your free trial at [URL]. First report is ready in 5 minutes."

Write the script in Google Docs first. Read it out loud. If you stumble over a sentence, rewrite it. Synthesia's avatar will stumble on the same words.

## Step 2: Generate the Presenter in Synthesia

Pick a pre-built avatar that matches your brand — friendly for B2C, professional for B2B. Paste the script. Select a voice that sounds natural, not robotic. The "natural" voice options in Synthesia's 160-language library have improved dramatically in 2026.

**Settings that matter:**
- Speed: 0.9x (slightly slower than natural — gives the viewer time to absorb information)
- Background: Branded template or solid color with your logo
- Resolution: 1080p

Generate the full 2-minute video. This takes about 5 minutes. Download the MP4.

## Step 3: Layer the Animation in CapCut

This is where the magic happens. Import the Synthesia video into CapCut. You'll add four layers:

**Layer 1 — Text overlays:** Every time the avatar mentions a feature, pop a text label on screen. Use CapCut's "Text Animation" presets — "Typewriter" for feature reveals, "Pop Up" for statistics, "Slide In" for transitions.

**Layer 2 — Screen recordings:** Record your product in action (Loom or OBS, both free). Drop these clips over the avatar when demonstrating features. The avatar's audio keeps playing underneath. Nobody needs to see the presenter during a product demo.

**Layer 3 — Transitions:** CapCut's "Smooth Zoom" transition between sections. It signals to the viewer that we're moving from problem to solution, or from feature 1 to feature 2. Without transitions, the video feels like one long, uncut monologue.

**Layer 4 — Lower thirds:** Add your company name and the presenter's name in the first 5 seconds. After that, use lower thirds only for key statistics or quotes.

## Step 4: Sound Design (the Thing Everyone Skips)

AI-generated voices are improving rapidly, but they still lack the natural variations in tone and volume that make human speech engaging. You need three audio assets to fix this:

**Background music:** CapCut's built-in music library has a "Corporate Explainer" category. Pick something upbeat but not distracting. Set volume to 15%. The music should be felt, not heard.

**Sound effects:** Add a subtle "pop" or "whoosh" when text overlays appear. It draws the viewer's eye to the new information. CapCut's "Sound Effects" library has hundreds of free options.

**Silence:** Yes, silence. After the hook and before the CTA, add 1 second of complete silence. It resets the viewer's attention for the final ask. Most editors fill every frame with sound. The silence is what makes the CTA hit.

## Step 5: Export and Review

Export at 1080p, 30fps. Watch the video twice: once with sound, once without. The silent run reveals whether the visual story holds up without narration. If a section feels empty, add a text overlay or screen recording there.

**What I'd do differently next time:**
- Record the screen demos before writing the script. It's easier to narrate a product demo you've already recorded than to script the demo and then try to match the recording to it
- Use CapCut's "Auto Captions" feature on the exported video. It generates subtitles with 90%+ accuracy and makes the video accessible to viewers watching without sound (most social media views)
